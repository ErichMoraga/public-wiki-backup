Accord

  • 6th generation (1998-2002) EX & LX
    • Panda is not green
  • 7th generation (2003-2007) EX & LX
    • Panda is not green
    • EX V6 model has traction control
  • 8th generation (2008-2012) EX & LX
    • Panda is green
    • Traction control and brake assist
  • 9th generation (2013-2017) EX & LX
    • 2016 Touring/EX + Honda Sensing Package (openpilot Status: Unsupported)
      • Can be ported (bounty: $2,000 + $500 iamwill)
      • Same JAE connector as the Civic. Braking is the same; steering is done over a 9600 baud LIN connection marked as EPSTOLKAS and LKASTOEPS.
      • Connecting Panda to Comma Neo
  • 10th generation (2018-present)
    • Appears to use the same Bosch ADAS hardware as 2017+ CR-V and 2017+ Civic Hatcback
    • Untested. Be the first! Join our Slack and ask for help in #honda.

Civic

  • 8th generation (2006-2011) EX & LX
    • Panda is not green (2007 EX)
  • 9th generation (2012-2015) EX & LX
    • Panda is green (2013 EX)
    • Traction control and brake assist
  • 10th generation (2016-present) EX & LX
    • Models w/Honda Sensing (openpilot status: supported)
      • NEO Installation: How to Access JAE Connector Civic 2016/2017
      • Due to limitations in steering firmware, steering is disabled below 12 mph
      • Hatchback does not use the same JAE 20-pin connector and is not yet publicly supported. Port: in progress. Giraffe: Custom. In progress. Method: Stock ACC, OpenPilot steering.
        • The 12-pin mating connector is a proprietary connector manufactured for Honda by JAE and is not available for purchase. The closed match to this connector is one designed by TE which can be modified to fit the male and female sides.
          • https://www.digikey.com/product-detail/en/te-connectivity-amp-connectors/2-1318118-6/A29798-ND/664517
            • Requires 12 contacts: https://www.digikey.com/product-detail/en/te-connectivity-amp-connectors/1318105-1/A29318CT-ND/4430538
          • Right angle header: https://www.digikey.com/product-detail/en/te-connectivity-amp-connectors/1376020-2/A29825-ND/664544
          • Straight header: https://www.digikey.com/product-detail/en/te-connectivity-amp-connectors/1318126-2/A29812-ND/664531

Clarity

  • (2017-present)
    • 20p JAE connector with extra CAN line into camera
    • Pinout here: [1]

CR-V

  • 4th generation (2012-2016)
    • 2015-2016 Touring (openpilot status: supported)
      • Due to the lack of "low-speed follow", openpilot can only be enabled above 25 mph
  • 5th generation (2017-present) EX, EX-L, and Touring
    • Radar, camera, and camera connector changed in this generation from 20p to proprietary 12p JAE connector
    • Radar does not send track data via CAN
    • Has "low-speed follow" like Honda Civic, steering appears to work down to 12mph
    • Will require different radar (or radar firmware) or no radar requirement by openpilot to be 100% compatible
    • Port: in progress. Giraffe: Custom. In progress. Method: Stock ACC, OpenPilot steering (at this time)

Fit

  • 2nd generation (2009-2014)
    • Panda is not green (2013 Sport).

ILX

  • (2016-present)
    • Models w/AcuraWatch Plus (openpilot status: supported)
      • Due to the lack of "low-speed follow", openpilot can only be enabled above 25 mph

MDX

  • (2017-present) AcuraWatch(AEB/LKA/ACC) is standard.
    • ACC is limited between 20 mph and 95 mph
    • Blind spot detection optional (enabled above 20 mph)
    • No direct CAN line from CAM to radar
    • Camera Pinout here http://i.imgur.com/cba3unF.png
    • 8 pin connector into CAM, differs from other hondas

Odyssey

  • 5th generation (2018-present) (openpilot status: supported)
    • Same 20p JAE connector as Honda Civic, but camera connector is UPSIDE DOWN
    • Giraffe doesn't fit into camera due to ^^. However, an adapter can be made using parts linked in Civic above. If using OpenPilot to drive, the Giraffe works since camera not needed.
    • Panda through Giraffe works fine and signaling looks similar to other Hondas
    • Standard OBD-II port seems to have a gateway and Panda doesn't work through it
    • No low-speed follow

Pilot

  • 3rd generation (2016-present) (openpilot status: Port In Progress)
    • There is a report that the 2016 Pilot has a different connector with 14pins
    • Has same 20p JAE connector as Honda Civic, all ACC/LKAS traffic over CAN, most likely has Nidec hardware
    • No low-speed follow

RDX

  • (2016-present) AcuraWatch (AEB/LKA/ACC) is available on all trims. (openpilot status: supported)
    • Same 20p JAE connector as Honda Civic, different pinout http://i.imgur.com/4XVExPo.png
    • No low-speed follow
    • Blind spot detection optional (enabled above 6 mph)

Ridgeline

  • 2nd generation (2017-present)
    • Same 20p JAE connector as Honda Civic
    • No low-speed follow

RLX

  • 1st generation (2015-2017)
    • Same 20p JAE connector as Honda Civic
    • Pinout here: http://i.imgur.com/ZmQvBsg.png
    • 3 CANs

TLX

  • 1st generation (2015-2017)
    • Same 20p JAE connector as Honda Civic
    • Pinout here: http://i.imgur.com/stf3ABh.png
    • 3 CANs + 2x LIN, F-CAN 1 / 2 and ACC CAN on Camera
